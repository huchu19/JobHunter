# Common mistakes (and how to avoid them)

The mistakes below cost applicants weeks of wasted effort — or a refused
visa. All of them are avoidable.

## Applying to companies that cannot sponsor

The most expensive mistake: spending interview cycles on a company that is
not on the
[register of licensed sponsors](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/register-of-licensed-sponsors-workers).
This entire app exists to prevent it — the board auto-verifies every company
you track, and the Sponsors tab searches the live register. Treat an
unverified company as a question to resolve in the **first** conversation,
not the last.

## Confusing trading names with registered names

The register lists **legal/registered names**, which often differ from the
brand you know. "DeepMind" sponsors as "DeepMind Technologies Limited"; a
startup may sponsor under a holding company. Before concluding a company
cannot sponsor, search variations of the name — the fuzzy matching in this
app helps, but a recruiter can confirm the registered entity in one email.

## Ignoring the going rate

Meeting the headline **£41,700** threshold is not enough if your occupation
code's **going rate** is higher — software engineering rates, for instance,
can sit well above the general threshold. An offer below the going rate
fails, full stop. Check
[the salary rules](https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa/your-job) against
the exact occupation code on your CoS.

## Leaving too little time

- You can apply **up to 3 months** before your start date — use that window.
- A company without a licence needs **~8 weeks** to get one before it can
  even assign your CoS.
- Graduating students switching from a Student visa: apply **before** your
  current permission expires, from inside the UK.

## Assuming sponsorship guarantees the visa

The CoS is the employer's half. You still need to clear salary, English,
maintenance funds, TB/criminal-record checks where applicable — and pay the
fees. Run the [eligibility checklist](/guides/eligibility-checklist) before
you celebrate.

## Money slip-ups

- The **£1,270 maintenance** must sit in your account for **28 consecutive
  days** ending within 31 days of applying — a transfer the week before
  does not count (unless your employer certifies maintenance instead).
- Budget the **Immigration Health Surcharge up front**: £1,035 × visa years,
  paid at application. A 3-year visa means £3,105 on top of the fee.

## Going quiet after rejection or job loss

If your employment ends, your visa is usually **curtailed to roughly 60
days** — that window is for finding a new licensed sponsor and applying to
update your visa, not for waiting. Track follow-ups and deadlines on the
board so nothing slips.
