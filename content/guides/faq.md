# Sponsorship FAQ

Short answers to the questions every visa-needing applicant asks. Figures
are as of June 2026 — verify current numbers on
[gov.uk](https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa) before acting.

## How much does it all cost?

- **Application fee (outside the UK):** £819 (up to 3 years) / £1,618 (over 3)
- **Application fee (inside the UK):** £943 (up to 3 years) / £1,865 (over 3)
- **Immigration salary list roles:** reduced to £628 / £1,235
- **Immigration Health Surcharge:** £1,035 per year, paid up front
- **Maintenance:** show £1,270 held for 28 days (or employer certifies it)

Full current pricing:
[how much it costs](https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa/how-much-it-costs).

## Can I switch from a Student or Graduate visa?

Yes — from inside the UK, once you have a CoS from a licensed sponsor. Decisions
on in-country applications take about **8 weeks** (standard service). New
entrants (including recent graduates under 26) may qualify under the lower
salary threshold.

## Can I change employer on a Skilled Worker visa?

Yes, but the new employer must also be a licensed sponsor and assign you a new
CoS, and you must
[update your visa](https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa/update-visa-if-change-job-or-employer)
**before** starting the new job. The same applies to significant role changes
at the same company.

## What happens if I lose my job?

Your permission is usually curtailed to **around 60 days** (or your visa end
date, if sooner). Within that window you can find another licensed sponsor and
apply to update your visa. This is why keeping your pipeline warm — even after
an offer — is worth it.

## Can my family come with me?

Usually yes — partners and children can apply as **dependants**, each paying
their own fee and Immigration Health Surcharge, and each showing additional
maintenance funds.

## How long until I can settle permanently?

The Skilled Worker route leads to **indefinite leave to remain after 5
years** of continuous residence, subject to ongoing salary and absence
requirements. After ILR, citizenship is typically possible a year later.

## Does a sponsor-register match guarantee they'll sponsor me?

No. The register (which this app verifies against) tells you the company
**can** sponsor — it holds an A-rated Worker licence. Whether they **will**
sponsor for a given role is a budget and policy decision. The register match
turns "can you sponsor?" from a hope into a negotiation.
