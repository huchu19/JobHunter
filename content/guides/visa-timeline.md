# The Skilled Worker visa timeline

From accepting an offer to holding the visa usually takes **4–15 weeks** end to
end. Here is the journey stage by stage, and where the time actually goes.

## 1. Offer accepted → Certificate of Sponsorship (1–4 weeks)

Once you accept an offer, your employer assigns you a **Certificate of
Sponsorship (CoS)** — an electronic record with a reference number, not a paper
document. Employers who already hold a sponsor licence and have CoS allocation
available can assign one in days. If they need to request more allocation from
the Home Office, expect this stage to stretch.

> Tip: ask your recruiter early whether the company already holds a sponsor
> licence and has CoS allocation in hand. This is the single biggest variable
> in the whole timeline.

## 2. Visa application (a few days)

With the CoS reference you apply online. You can apply **up to 3 months before
your job start date** — the date on your CoS. You will pay the application fee
and the **Immigration Health Surcharge (£1,035 per year of visa, as of
June 2026)** at this point. See
[Skilled Worker visa: how much it costs](https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa/how-much-it-costs)
for current figures.

## 3. Identity verification / biometrics (0–2 weeks)

Most applicants verify their identity with the **UK Immigration: ID Check**
phone app. Some applicants are asked to attend a visa application centre to
give fingerprints and a photo instead — if so, appointment availability adds
time.

## 4. Home Office decision (3–8 weeks standard)

Published service standards (as of June 2026):

- About **3 weeks** when applying from **outside** the UK
- About **8 weeks** when applying from **inside** the UK (extending or switching)

Where available, the **priority service (5 working days)** and
**super-priority service (next working day)** can shorten the wait for an
extra fee — see
[faster decisions on gov.uk](https://www.gov.uk/faster-decision-visa-settlement).

## What can slow it down

- The employer must apply for a sponsor licence first (add **8+ weeks**)
- A visa application centre appointment instead of the ID Check app
- Missing documents — especially evidence of maintenance funds or English
- Peak-season application volumes

The official overview lives at
[gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa](https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa). Every
company page in this app also shows this timeline estimate in context.
