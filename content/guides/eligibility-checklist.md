# Eligibility checklist

Work through this list before you invest time in applications. Figures are
current as of June 2026 — always confirm against
[gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa](https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa) before
relying on them.

## The essentials

- **A job offer from a licensed sponsor.** The employer must appear on the
  [register of licensed sponsors](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/register-of-licensed-sponsors-workers)
  with a Worker licence. This app's sponsor verification checks exactly this
  register — the Sponsors tab searches it live.
- **A Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)** assigned by that employer for the
  specific role, issued no more than 3 months before you apply.
- **An eligible occupation.** The role must be skilled enough to qualify
  (broadly graduate-level work; some medium-skilled roles qualify only via the
  immigration salary list or temporary shortage list).
- **Salary at or above the threshold.** Usually the higher of **£41,700 per
  year** or the **going rate** for your occupation code. See
  [your job requirements](https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa/your-job).
- **English at level B1** (intermediate) — proven by a degree taught in
  English, an approved test, or nationality of a majority-English-speaking
  country.

## The lower salary threshold

You may qualify at **£33,400 per year** (still subject to a reduced going
rate) if, for example:

- You are a **new entrant** — under 26, a recent graduate, or in professional
  training
- You hold a **PhD relevant to the job** (STEM PhDs get the biggest discount)
- The job is on the **immigration salary list**
- Different rules apply to some **healthcare and education** roles on national
  pay scales

## Money and documents

- **£1,270 in savings**, held for 28 consecutive days before applying, unless
  your employer certifies maintenance on the CoS
- A valid **passport**
- A **TB test certificate** if you are applying from a
  [listed country](https://www.gov.uk/tb-test-visa)
- A **criminal record certificate** for some roles (mostly health, care, and
  education)

## Quick self-check

1. Is the company on the sponsor register? (Search it in this app.)
2. Does the offered salary clear both £41,700 and the occupation's going rate?
3. Can you evidence English at B1?
4. Have you held £1,270 for 28 days, or will the employer certify maintenance?

If all four are yes, you are very likely eligible — the rest is paperwork.
