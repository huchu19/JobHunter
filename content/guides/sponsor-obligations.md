# What sponsors must do (and why it matters to you)

Understanding the employer's side of sponsorship helps you read between the
lines of "we don't sponsor" — often it means "we don't want the admin", not
"it's impossible".

## The sponsor licence

To hire you, the employer needs a **sponsor licence** from the Home Office —
see [UK visa sponsorship for employers](https://www.gov.uk/uk-visa-sponsorship-employers).
They must prove they are a genuine organisation, nominate people to manage
sponsorship internally, and pay a licence fee. A new licence application
typically takes around **8 weeks** to be decided.

## A-rating vs B-rating

Newly licensed sponsors get an **A-rating**. Sponsors who fail their duties
can be downgraded to a **B-rating** — they then must follow an improvement
action plan and **cannot issue new Certificates of Sponsorship** until they
are upgraded back to A. This app's sponsor search covers **A-rated Worker
sponsors**, so a match means the company can actually sponsor today.

## Ongoing duties

A licensed sponsor must:

- **Assign a CoS** only for genuine vacancies that meet the skill and salary
  rules
- **Report to the Home Office** within set deadlines when a sponsored worker
  does not turn up, is absent without permission, changes role or salary, or
  leaves
- **Keep records** — right-to-work checks, contact details, salary evidence
- **Cooperate with compliance visits**, announced or not

## What sponsorship costs the employer

Beyond the licence fee, employers pay a fee per CoS and usually the
**Immigration Skills Charge** (typically £1,000 per sponsored worker per
year for medium and large companies, less for small ones and charities).
Some of these charges legally **cannot be passed on to you** — a company
that asks you to repay the Immigration Skills Charge risks losing its
licence.

## Why this matters for your search

- Sponsorship is real, recurring work for the employer — target companies
  that already sponsor (they are on the
  [register](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/register-of-licensed-sponsors-workers)
  and have done the admin before).
- A company "not on the register" can still sponsor you — but they must get a
  licence first, adding roughly two months. Factor that into your timeline
  (see the [visa timeline guide](/guides/visa-timeline)).
- "Sponsoring someone does not guarantee that they'll get a visa" — the
  Home Office decides the visa itself.
